Workout Splits

Explore the different types of workout splits and find one that suits your fitness goals.

Upper/Lower Split
What is an Upper/Lower Split?

An upper/lower split involves alternating between upper body workouts and lower body workouts. This method is efficient for balancing muscle groups and allowing sufficient recovery time.

Pros and Cons

Pros: Balanced muscle development, allows for focus on specific areas. Cons: Requires more gym visits per week.

Sample Routines

Example routine:
- Day 1: Upper Body
- Day 2: Lower Body
- Day 3: Rest
- Day 4: Upper Body
- Day 5: Lower Body
- Day 6: Rest
- Day 7: Rest

Push/Pull/Legs
What is a Push/Pull/Legs Split?

The Push/Pull/Legs split divides workouts into pushing movements (chest, shoulders, triceps), pulling movements (back, biceps), and leg exercises.

Pros and Cons

Pros: Comprehensive, allows for focused muscle group training. Cons: Can be time-consuming, especially for beginners.

Sample Routines

Example routine:
- Day 1: Push
- Day 2: Pull
- Day 3: Legs
- Day 4: Rest
- Day 5: Push
- Day 6: Pull
- Day 7: Legs

Full Body Workout
What is a Full Body Workout?

A full body workout engages multiple muscle groups in a single session. It is ideal for those with limited time and helps in maintaining overall muscle balance.

Pros and Cons

Pros: Time-efficient, great for beginners. Cons: May not provide enough volume for advanced muscle growth.

Sample Routines

Example routine:
- Day 1: Full Body
- Day 2: Rest
- Day 3: Full Body
- Day 4: Rest
- Day 5: Full Body
- Day 6: Rest
- Day 7: Rest
